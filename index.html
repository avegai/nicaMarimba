The marimba is believed to have pre-Columbian origins, with roots in the indigenous cultures of the Mesoamerican region but there is another version, which states that the marimba was brought to the continent by the African slaves. The Nicaraguan marimba does not look that different compared to other marimbas in the region like the Guatemalan or the Colombian one, for example. However, sonically it has an extra amount of crunch due to the gourd resonators with membranes made out of pig intestines. I had the privilege to work with 2 fantastic marimba players from this new generation in Nicaragua, Carlos Luis Mejia and Evenor Gonzalez. Both of them have stretched the possibilities of the instrument, while staying true to its sound. They played for la Cuneta Son Machin and Momotombo respectively. When recording the instrument and putting together this set of sounds, we dug deep into traditional sounding riffs, patterns and motifs while staying true to the traditional sound of the instrument.
